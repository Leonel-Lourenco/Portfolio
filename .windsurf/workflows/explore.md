## Objective
Explore unknowns with structured, time-boxed investigation to reduce risk before committing to implementation.

## Requirements
1. **Define the Question**
   - What specific uncertainty are we investigating?
   - What would success look like?
   - What are the key risks we're trying to mitigate?

2. **Set Boundaries**
   - Time limit (e.g., 30 min, 1 hour, half day)
   - Scope limit (specific questions only)
   - Output expectation (decision, prototype, or "need more info")

3. **Explore Systematically**
   - Start with documentation and existing examples
   - Build minimal proof-of-concept if needed
   - Test assumptions with real code, not speculation
   - Document findings as you go

4. **Conclude Decisively**
   - Summarize what was learned
   - Provide clear recommendation: proceed, pivot, or abandon
   - Identify remaining unknowns if any
   - Estimate effort for full implementation

## Spike Outcomes
- **Proceed**: Approach is viable, here's how to implement
- **Pivot**: Original approach won't work, but alternative X will
- **Abandon**: This isn't feasible, here's why
- **Extend**: Need more investigation, here's what's still unknown

## When to Use
- New technology or library evaluation
- Unclear requirements needing clarification
- Performance feasibility questions
- Integration complexity assessment
- "Is this even possible?" questions

## Deliverable
Clear recommendation with supporting evidence, not production code. The goal is informed decision-making, not implementation.
